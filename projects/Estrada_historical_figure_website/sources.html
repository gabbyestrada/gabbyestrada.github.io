Albert Einstein left a big impact on the science and technological world and was sure to have his findings last many decades. Including inspiring others to be so interested, they write articles and papers of the infamous scientist. There are countless papers written on Einstein and most of these are very reliable sources.

On August 2nd in 1939, amidst the the rising tensions of WWII, Albert Einstein writes a letter of warning to President Franklin D. Roosevelt. (Einstein 1) The image above shows this letter. Einstein states in his letter, “ Certain aspects of the situation which has arisen seem to call for watchfulness and, if necessary, quick action...” (1) He explains what Germany is developing and makes a point of delivering just how near into their future this can be, advising America to be proactive. However, what isn’t stated in the letter is how Einstein has so much knowledge of the capabilities of these bombs, he “played almost no role in the development of the atomic bomb but whose discoveries led to it” such as the theory of relativity. (Rosen 1)

In 1999 Time Magazine named Albert Einstein Person of The Century for all that he has done. In his article, Frederic Golden talks about Einstein’s impact and all that he has done. Golden talks of his early life, stating, “at age 15, Einstein staged his first great rebellion” and goes on to explaining how Einstein’s younger years went. (2) Also going on to describing how moral Einstein was, writing, “At the height of World War I, he risked the Kaiser's wrath by signing an antiwar petition.” (3) Overall Frederic Golden embodied Albert Einstein as a worthy person and did not fail to mention the depths of his intellect, describing him as “ the genius among geniuses who discovered, merely by thinking about it, that the universe was not as it seemed.” (1) Golden made sure to fully justify the reasoning of naming Albert Einstein Person of The Century.

The book originally published in 1935, The World as I See It was a book written by Albert Einstein himself. Many people know Einstein as the genius who has done the many feats for the science and technological world. This book goes into who he really was and provides an insight to how his mind works. It goes through his philosophies delving straight into the age old question: “what is the meaning of life, or of all organic life together?” (Einstein 2) Einstein’s book also goes into views of the tension between German Nazis and Jews, politics, and other topics. (Einstein 43, 81, 90)

The Collected Papers of Albert Einstein is look inside Albert Einstein's scientific letters and documents, but rather than in a museum, on the web. This website allows anyone to look through his findings and intellectual insights. According to an article written by Dan Coleman, “To date, 13 volumes of Einstein’s writing (or 7,000 pages from 2,900 documents) have been published, and they all now appear in electronic format.” (1) This website is for anyone who is interested in the development of technology being that Einstein played a key role. The website is very detailed with his papers and includes interesting information vital to Einstein researchers.
